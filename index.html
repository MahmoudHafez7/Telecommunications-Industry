Intro

Electrical Telegraph (1838):

Samuel B. Morse, with his friends Alfred Vail and Leonard Gale, invented the electrical Telegraph. It gave businesses the ability to communicate without being physically close to the person you are contacting.

Telephone (1876):

The telephone was discovered by a Canadian engineer, Abraham Alexander Graham Bell. The invention of the telephone was Canada's most significant contribution to the telecommunications industry.

Television (1927):

Phillip Farnsworth found the television. Twenty-five years after the discovery of TV, the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation (CBC) broadcasted in Montreal and Toronto.

Mobile Phone (1973):

Martin Cooper discovered the mobile phone when he completed the first phone call through a cellular phone. It have infiltrated and revolutionized the modern world.

Internet (1983):

Robert Khan launched the internet. This invention has opened new types of businesses like e-commerce. It also gave companies new ways of communicating and reaching customers.

Email (1991):

As a byproduct of the World Wide Web, email was introduced to the world in 1991, and it has vastly changed our lives.

Technological changes

Voice over Internet Protocol(VoIP) and Video Conferencing:

VOIP is a technology that transmits voice notes over the internet to other areas. Businesses used this innovation to solve their problems of communication expenses that have increased massively.

Social Media:

Social Media is a platform that makes people able to interact with the web to create contentment. People consider Social Media as one of the greatest inventions of the 21st century. Social Media have changed the way people communicate, receive, and gather information and their lifestyles.

Innovations and Improvements in Mobile Phones:

The improvements in cellphone technology have radically changed the way that consumers use them. In 2007, Steve Jobs launched his new revolutionary iPhone, and Apple paved the way for modern smartphones’ aesthetics.

New processes

Cellular Phone Manufacturing: The Role of Artificial intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is one of the most innovative technologies in recent times and is taking mobile technology to the next level. It enhances user experience with various features like face recognition, voice commands, image labeling. Smart mobile developers are introducing AI technology in their manufacturing process to make mobile phones more smart & intelligent.

Big Data: New Age of Marketing of Cellular phones

Big data is the study of large data sets to reveal patterns, trends, and behaviors. The intense information about the user’s behavior, including the likes, expectations, needs, demands, etc., enables Mobile companies to build an accurate and compatible marketing strategy in this highly competitive market

New attitudes and trends

Environmental awareness:

The telecommunication industry has damaged the environment heavily in the last couple of years. Researchers have found that by 2040, smartphones and data centers will have the most massive carbon footprint in the tech industry.

Health concerns

Mobile phones emit a form of electromagnetic radiation called radiofrequency energy, which is absorbed by human bodies. In 2011, the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classified RF radiation as possibly carcinogenic to humans. Mobile phones also influence our nervous system. Phones may cause headaches, decreased attention, shortness of temper, sleep disorders, and depression, mostly among teenagers.

Social Trends

The widespread adoption of the cell phone as a tool of communication and entertainment has revolutionized society, redefining patterns of social contact and relationships among individuals. Addiction to social networking, dating apps, texting, and messaging can extend to the point where virtual, online friends become more important than real-life relationships.

Future

5G Wireless Technology

One of the enormous changes that will happen in telecommunications is 5G, which is an innovation. According to CNN, 5G is a new wireless technology expected to change people’s lives and work. It is a unique network that will help connect virtually everyone and create a new technological advancement.

Internet of Things (IoT)

One of the vast innovations that we will witness in the next couple of years is the Internet of Things. Internet of Things refers to making physical objects connected to the internet and sharing data to it, without any human intervention.
